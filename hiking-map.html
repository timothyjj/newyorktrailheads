Hiking Map

Looking for a place to hike in (or near) the Adirondack Mountains? Check out the points on this map for several hiking options around the Lake George/Saratoga region of New York State. Note that hikes here range in difficulty levels from easy to moderate to difficult. Note that when traveling into the Adirondacks, be sure to check trail conditions! Snow comes early at the end of the year and can stick around into late spring, followed by mud season.

Easy hikes (short distance, slight elevation gain, reasonable to inexperienced hikers and families with young children):

  • Shelving Rock Mountain
  • Sleeping Beauty Mountain
  • Inman Pond
  • Some portions of Palmertown Range (Moreau Lake State Park)
Moderate hikes (short to medium distance, medium elevation gain, reasonable for hikers with some experience and older kids):
  • Palmertown Range
  • Pilot Knob Preserve
  • Hadley Mountain
  • Prospect Mountain
Difficult hikes (medium to long distance, heavy elevation gain, best for experienced hikers and kids in teenage years and up)
  • Buck Mountain
  • Cat & Thomas Mountains
  • Tongue Mountain Range
  • Black Mountain
